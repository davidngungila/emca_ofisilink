## Overall Structure

### 1. Header Section
- Organization name (centered, bold, uppercase)
- Meeting type/category (centered, bold, uppercase)
- "MINUTES OF THE [TYPE] MEETING" (centered, uppercase)
- Date (formatted as DD/MM/YYYY)
- Venue/Location

**Example:**
```
ELCT ND SACCOS
BOARD MEETING
MINUTES OF THE BOARD MEETING - QUARTER II OF 2025
28/07/2025
LUTHERAN UHURU HOTEL & CONFERENCE CENTRE
```

### 2. Opening Section
**Agenda No. 1: Opening of Meeting**

- Reference Number (Ref. No. 1)
- Opening time
- Opening prayer leader name
- Hymn number (if applicable)
- Scripture reading reference
- Opening remarks/theme
- Closing prayer leader name

**Template:**
```
AGENDA NO. 1: OPENING OF MEETING

Ref. No. 1
The meeting was opened at [TIME] with prayer led by [NAME], 
followed by hymn [NUMBER] and scripture reading from [BIBLE REFERENCE].

[Opening remarks about the theme - e.g., working with love, unity, etc.]

Opening was concluded with prayer led by [NAME].
```

### 3. Attendance Section
**Agenda No. 2: Attendance Review and Agenda Review**

- Reference Number (Ref. No. 2)
- Quorum verification statement
- Attendance count breakdown:
  - Board Members present
  - Member Representatives present
  - Committee Members present

**Attendees Table:**
| No. | Name | Position/Title |
|-----|------|----------------|
| 1   | ...  | ...            |

**Invitees Table:**
| No. | Name | Position/Title |
|-----|------|----------------|
| 1   | ...  | ...            |

**Agenda Items List:**
- Numbered list of all agenda items to be discussed

**Template:**
```
AGENDA NO. 2: ATTENDANCE REVIEW AND AGENDA REVIEW

Ref. No. 2
The Chairperson reviewed attendance of Board Members considering 
the [ORGANIZATION] Constitution Article [X] which states "Board 
meetings will be valid if members in attendance are half of all 
Board Members". The quorum was satisfied with the attendance of 
[NUMBER] Board Members, [NUMBER] Member Representative(s), and 
[NUMBER] member(s) of the [COMMITTEE] Committee.

ATTENDEES:
[Table with Name and Position/Title]

INVITEES:
[Table with Name and Position/Title]

AGENDA ITEMS:
1. Opening of Meeting
2. Attendance Review and Agenda Review
3. [Other agenda items...]
```

### 4. Previous Meeting Minutes
**Agenda No. 3: Reading of Previous Meeting Minutes**

- Reference Number (Ref. No. 3)
- Date of previous meeting
- Confirmation statement that minutes are accurate

**Template:**
```
AGENDA NO. 3: READING OF PREVIOUS MEETING MINUTES

Ref. No. 3
The Chairperson presented members with minutes from the previous 
meeting dated [DATE]. Members confirmed that these accurately 
reflect what was discussed in those meetings.
```

## Follow-ups from Previous Meetings

**Agenda No. 4: Follow-ups from Meeting Dated [DATE]**

This section tracks action items and resolutions from previous meetings.

### Table Format:

| Ref. No. | DESCRIPTION | BOARD ORDERS/RESOLUTIONS | IMPLEMENTATION STATUS |
|----------|-------------|--------------------------|----------------------|
| Ref. 1   | [Description of item] | [Board order/resolution] | [Status: Completed/In Progress/Pending] |
| Ref. 2   | ... | ... | ... |

### Column Descriptions:

1. **Ref. No. (Reference Number):** 
   - Sequential reference number (Ref. 1, Ref. 2, etc.)
   - Corresponds to agenda item number from previous meeting

2. **DESCRIPTION (MAELEZO YA JAMBO):**
   - Brief description of the matter/item from previous meeting
   - Should be clear and specific

3. **BOARD ORDERS/RESOLUTIONS (MAAGIZO YA BODI):**
   - Specific directives or resolutions made by the Board
   - Should include actionable items and responsibilities

4. **IMPLEMENTATION STATUS (UTEKELEZAJI):**
   - Current status of implementation:
     - **Completed** - Task fully implemented
     - **In Progress** - Task ongoing
     - **Pending** - Task not yet started
     - **Deferred** - Task postponed

**Template:**
```
AGENDA NO. 4: FOLLOW-UPS FROM MEETING DATED [DATE]

Ref. No. 4

| Ref. No. | DESCRIPTION | BOARD ORDERS/RESOLUTIONS | IMPLEMENTATION STATUS |
|----------|-------------|--------------------------|----------------------|
| Ref. 1   | Share Certificates | All members who have completed 
          Shares of TSh.500,000/- should be given Share Certificates. | Completed |
| Ref. 2   | Mwanza Office | Investment Committee should review 
          costs for the office space along with office infrastructure. | In Progress |
| ...      | ... | ... | ... |
```

## Agenda Items Format

Each agenda item follows this structure:

**Template:**
```
AGENDA NO. [NUMBER]: [AGENDA TITLE]

Ref. No. [NUMBER]
[Presented by: NAME] (if applicable)

DISCUSSION:
[Detailed notes of what was discussed, presentations made, 
data shared, concerns raised, questions asked, etc.]

RESOLUTION/DECISION:
[Clear statement of decisions made, votes taken, agreements reached]

BOARD ORDER NO. [NUMBER]:
The Board issued the following directives to Management:
1. [Directive 1]
2. [Directive 2]
3. [Directive 3]
...
```

### Example - Manager's Report:
```
AGENDA NO. 5: MANAGER'S REPORT

Ref. No. 5
The Secretary/Acting Manager presented a performance report of 
the Organization for the period January to June 2025, showing 
implementation of the 2025 work plan for all branches with 
analysis of Shares, Savings, Deposits, Loans, Revenue and 
Expenditure, as follows:

DISCUSSION:
- Work efficiency: Number of members increased from 17,656 to 
  18,537 (+881).
- Shares, Savings and Deposits continued to grow.
- Loans increased compared to 2024.
- Digital services improved through SMS system for sending 
  transaction notifications to members.
...

RESOLUTION/DECISION:
The Board reviewed and noted the report with appreciation for 
the progress made.

BOARD ORDER NO. 1:
The Board issued the following directives to Management:
1. Members who have not completed their Shares should continue 
   to be called to remind them to complete. Continue to educate 
   Members about the importance of placing Shares, Savings 
   together with use of other Organization services.
2. Prepare monthly reports of Members who save every month. 
   Additionally, the Chairperson has encouraged Board leaders 
   to save every month. Conduct thorough investigation of Members 
   who joined the Organization more than three years ago and have 
   not completed their Shares, write them letters or send SMS to 
   remind them to complete their Shares.
3. ...
```

## Committee Reports Format

Committee reports follow a similar structure:

**Template:**
```
AGENDA NO. [NUMBER]: [COMMITTEE NAME] COMMITTEE REPORT

Ref. No. [NUMBER]
[Report presented by: NAME]

REPORT SUMMARY:
[Overview of the committee's activities, findings, 
recommendations, statistics, etc.]

BOARD DECISIONS:
The Board made the following decisions:
1. [Decision 1]
2. [Decision 2]
3. ...
```

### Example - Loans Committee Report:
```
AGENDA NO. 6.1: LOANS COMMITTEE REPORT

Ref. No. 6.1
Report presented by: [Committee Chairperson Name]

REPORT SUMMARY:
The Committee Chairperson presented a report explaining the 
reality of implementation of the work plan in lending and loan 
repayment as follows:

1. Loan Disbursement
   - Budgeted: TSh. 17,788,025,000/=
   - Actual Disbursed: TSh. 15,733,436,556/= (88% of budget)
   - Shortfall: TSh. 2,054,588,444/= (12%)
   
   Loans exceeding expectations:
   - Emergency Loans (144%)
   - Institution Loans (150%)
   - Construction Loans (236%)
   ...

2. Loan Repayments
   - Budgeted: TSh. 18,382,977,100/=
   - Actual: TSh. 13,370,755,409/= (73% of budget)
   - Shortfall: TSh. 5,012,221,691/= (27%)
   ...

3. Management Comments:
   - Continue to lend clean and safe loans that are repaid on 
     time by following lending policies and regulations.
   - Strengthen loan repayment follow-up for defaulters through 
     internal working teams and court brokers.

4. Loans Committee Comments:
   - Some products such as Kibati, Weekend and Gulio should be 
     reviewed again regarding their effectiveness.
   - Continue to educate members about lending criteria and 
     conditions.
   ...

BOARD DECISIONS:

Decision No. 2 - Board:
The Board directed the Loans Committee as follows:
1. Provide a report on cases in court, together with preparing 
   a list of Persistent Defaulters.
2. Prepare a report on loans collected and owed by each Branch, 
   together with a list of defaulters who need to be questioned/
   demanded by the Board. Report on debt collectors.
3. Provide a report on the loan status of leaders (new and 
   those who have completed their term).
4. Make/classify improvement areas in the Loans Policy.
5. Conduct research on agricultural loans and guarantees that 
   can work well in Tabora.
```

## Best Practices

### 1. Reference Numbering
- Each agenda item should have a sequential reference number
- Format: "Ref. No. 1", "Ref. No. 2", etc.
- Reference numbers should be consistent and sequential

### 2. Decision Documentation
- All decisions should be clearly stated
- Use numbered lists for multiple directives/decisions
- Include responsible parties where applicable
- Include deadlines or timelines where relevant

### 3. Follow-up Tracking
- Every decision/resolution from previous meetings should be tracked
- Implementation status should be updated accurately
- Provide brief notes on progress or challenges

### 4. Language and Tone
- Use formal, professional language
- Be clear and concise
- Avoid jargon unless necessary (provide explanations)
- Use active voice where possible

### 5. Financial Data
- Always include currency (TSh., USD, etc.)
- Use consistent number formatting
- Include percentages for comparisons
- Show actual vs. budgeted where applicable

### 6. Dates and Times
- Use consistent date format (DD/MM/YYYY or DD MMM YYYY)
- Always include time in 12-hour format with AM/PM
- Include venue/location for all meetings

### 7. Attendance
- Separate Board Members from Invitees
- Include position/title for all attendees
- Verify quorum requirements are met

### 8. Action Items
- Link action items to specific agenda items
- Assign responsible parties clearly
- Include due dates where applicable
- Track implementation status

## Template Variables

When using the template in the system, these variables should be populated:

- `$meeting` - Meeting object with all details
- `$meeting->title` - Meeting title
- `$meeting->meeting_date` - Meeting date
- `$meeting->start_time` - Start time
- `$meeting->end_time` - End time
- `$meeting->venue` - Venue/location
- `$meeting->category_name` - Meeting category/type
- `$boardMembers` - Array of board member attendees
- `$invitees` - Array of invitee attendees
- `$agendas` - Collection of agenda items
- `$minutes` - Minutes object with closing details, AOB, etc.
- `$previousActions` - Collection of follow-up items from previous meetings
- `$committeeReports` - Array of committee reports (if applicable)
- `$internalAuditReport` - Internal audit report (if applicable)
- `$memberRepresentativeReport` - Member representative report (if applicable)

## Additional Notes

1. **Minutes Approval**: Minutes should be approved at the next meeting before being considered official.

2. **Distribution**: Approved minutes should be distributed to all Board Members and relevant stakeholders.

3. **Confidentiality**: Some meeting minutes may contain confidential information. Ensure proper access controls.

4. **Translation**: If minutes are prepared in one language but need to be in another, ensure accuracy of translation, especially for financial data and legal terms.

5. **Retention**: Maintain proper records retention policy for meeting minutes as per organizational and legal requirements.
